Design Tricks for Google Sites

🧭 Why This Matters

Google Sites is easy to use—but it can look cluttered or unreadable if you’re not careful. Here’s how to make your content shine, without touching any code.

🎯 Centered Layouts

  • Use the text box resize handles to manually center your text.
  • Avoid stretching your content across the full page width—it makes reading harder.
  • Keep your layout narrow, like reading a book.

🖋 Font Tips

  • Select legible fonts like Maven Pro or Spectral.
  • Use font size around 13–15 for body text.
  • Avoid bold or italic unless it adds emphasis.
  • Adjust line spacing to around 1.25 for comfort.

🎨 Color & Contrast

  • Use bright or white fonts on dark backgrounds for clarity.
  • Set links to a visible color and add a soft hover effect like #ffe066.
  • Avoid using broken or unreadable code icons—skip them if they don’t load right.

📚 Visual Structure

  • Use emojis at the top of each section to hint at the theme visually.
  • Apply different heading colors for sections—green for tips, yellow for examples, blue for intro, pink for wrap-up.
  • Skip dividers unless absolutely necessary—they clutter the flow.

🚀 Real-World Examples

Don’t use unrelated references—if your site is about creativity or writing, stick to those examples. It helps focus the message.

“Your layout is your stage. Design it to guide the eyes and invite curiosity.”
