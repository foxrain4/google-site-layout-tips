🛠️ Quick Layout Tips for Google Sites

Speed up design, stay consistent, and save yourself from layout frustration!

🧩 1. Use a Master Copy (Your Perfect Box)

Once you've made a textbox with ideal width, font size, spacing, alignment, etc., duplicate it instead of starting fresh each time.
Right-click the textbox → Duplicate, then just swap the content. Keeps spacing perfect with zero extra work.

📦 2. Resize Smartly: Use the Blue Grid Lines

Google Sites shows snap-to-blue-lines when resizing. Drag your textbox until it locks to the center — this ensures proper visual balance.

🏗️ 3. Use a Single Column Layout

Avoid multiple columns for text. Keep your layout in one clean vertical stack. Use line breaks or section padding to separate content instead of messy dividers.

🧙‍♂️ 4. Zoom Out for Easier Layout

Zoom your browser out (e.g., Ctrl + -) while resizing. This makes it easier to align boxes and keep proportions. Zoom back in to refine content.

🖼️ 5. Stack Text and Images Vertically

Put images above or below text, not side-by-side. This scales better on mobile and keeps everything centered naturally.

💾 6. Prebuilt Templates

Create a hidden "template" page with various pre-styled blocks — textboxes, headers, image layouts, etc. Duplicate the page for new sections and just change the content!

⚠️ Bonus Gotcha Warning

Google Sites sometimes messes up formatting when pasting from Google Docs or Word. To avoid hidden issues:

  • Paste into Notepad first, then into Google Sites
  • Or use Ctrl + Shift + V to paste without formatting

"Work smart, not just hard — your fingertips will thank you."
